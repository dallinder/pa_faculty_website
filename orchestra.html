Daniel Allinder is the Orchestra Director at Providence Hall High School. He has a Bachelor of Music Eduction, Double Bass from Southeast Missouri State University and has performed in symphonies, wind ensembles, and jazz groups. He has been directing Orchestra's for six years and performed internationally with the Southeast Symphony Orchestra in 2010 on a tour through China, which included a performance on the Great Wall of China. About our Program

The Providence Hall Orchestra offers several courses for string players. At the Junior High level there is a beginning and and advanced Orchestra sections and at the High School there is an intermediate and advanced section. The junior high orchestra performs at Lagoon in the Spring for their festival, while the High School take a tour every 2 years out of state. Our last trip was to Los Angeles, where students recieved a clinic from Los Angeles Philharmonic violinist Arrousiak Balatian. The High School also performs at the USHAA Orchestra Festival, where they have recieved Excellent ratings. High School students also have the opportuinity to perform at Solo and Ensemble in the Spring. We hope that you want to be apart of our successful and growing program!
